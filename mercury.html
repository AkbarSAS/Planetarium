Planet Overview

The Swift Planet

Mercury is the smallest planet in the Solar System and the closest to the Sun.

With extreme temperatures and a cratered surface, it resembles Earth's Moon but orbits far faster.

0.38x Earth Gravity
430°C Day Temperature
−180°C Night Temperature
88 Days / Year

Did You Know?

Mercury's Amazing Facts

Fastest Orbit

Mercury completes a full orbit around the Sun in just 88 days.

Double Sunrise

The Sun can rise, set, and rise again due to Mercury's unusual rotation speed relative to its orbit.

No Atmosphere

Mercury has almost no atmosphere, causing extreme temperature swings between day and night.

Iron Core

Over 70% of Mercury's mass is its massive metallic core.

Internal Structure

Layers of Mercury

Layer 01
Crust

A thin rocky layer covered in craters and ridges.

Layer 02
Mantle

A silicate layer surrounding the core.

Layer 03
Outer Core

Partially molten metal generating Mercury's weak magnetic field.

Layer 04
Inner Core

A large solid iron core making up most of the planet's mass.

Surface & Exosphere

Mercury's Thin Exosphere

Mercury has no true atmosphere but only a thin exosphere of sodium, oxygen, and hydrogen.

This allows solar wind to strike the surface directly, shaping its landscape.
