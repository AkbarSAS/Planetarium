Planet Overview

The Blue Marble

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbour life.

71% of Earth's surface is covered by water, giving it its iconic blue appearance from space.

4.5B Years Old

Earth's Amazing Facts

The Oceans

Earth's oceans contain 1.335 billion km³ of water.

Magnetic Field

Generated by the liquid iron outer core, it shields Earth from harmful solar winds.

Tectonics

Earth's crust is divided into moving plates that shape continents and cause earthquakes.

Atmosphere

Protects life from radiation and regulates temperature.

Internal Structure

Layers of the Earth

Layer 01
Crust

The outermost layer, ranging from 5 to 70 km thick.

Layer 02
Upper Mantle

Semi-solid rock that flows slowly over millions of years.

Layer 03
Lower Mantle

Dense and rigid rock under extreme pressure.

Layer 04
Outer Core

Liquid iron and nickel — generates Earth's magnetic field.

Layer 05
Inner Core

Solid metal sphere about the size of the Moon.

Bonus
Atmosphere

Earth's protective gas layer extending hundreds of kilometres above the surface.

Life Support System

Earth's Atmosphere

Earth's atmosphere is a thin but vital layer of gases that makes life possible. It keeps temperatures stable, allows us to breathe, and protects us from the Sun's harmful radiation.

The ozone layer, found in the stratosphere, filters out most of the Sun's damaging ultraviolet rays.
